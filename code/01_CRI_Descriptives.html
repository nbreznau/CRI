Descriptives

Sample

Our final sample is 73 teams including 162 researchers that submitted 1262 models. Of those teams, one did not conduct the hypothesis test based on a failed measurement invariance test, another had all their 8 models fail to converge and one other had one model out of several fail to converge. The result was 71 teams with numeric results of their hypothesis tests derived from 1253 models produced by 158 researchers in total with an average of 2.244 researchers per team. There were 161 participants in 73 teams that produced 1261 models, average of 17.274 models per team.

There were 16 teams that came to more than one conclusion. For example, finding support of the hypothesis for immigration stock models, but finding rejection of the hypothesis for immigration flow models. Therefore, we have 89 observations (results) in the team-level data that derive from 72 teams. We also have the conclusion from the study by Brady and Finnigan (2014) that provides an example of a state of the art study for testing this hypothesis included as team number 0 for comparison purposes.

Summary Stats

We no longer use this approach but leave these tables here in case of interest. The codebook is now our main summary statistics location.

Note that the N of 89 reflects the number of conclusions provided in total from 73 teams, with one of those conclusions having no results attached to it because the team concluded through measurement testing that the data were inappropriate. Thus, 88 is our team-level-results N for comparison.
